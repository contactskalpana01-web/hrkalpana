My Journey

From aspiring scientist to people leader.

My journey hasn't followed a straight line, and that's exactly what has made it meaningful.

Growing up in a Hindi-speaking household, I became determined to master English. Books and movies became my teachers, and I spent countless hours learning to adapt and grow in unfamiliar situations. This early experience unintentionally built the foundational skills that would shape my future.

My academic path actually began in Biochemistry, driven by dreams of working in healthcare and research. However, I soon realized that while I enjoyed understanding complex systems, I was far more fascinated by understanding people. That realization changed everything.

What started as an unexpected career shift into Human Resources eventually became a profession I genuinely love.

The Journey So Far

2017
Discovering HR

My first exposure to Human Resources came during an internship focused on recruitment and talent management.

For the first time, I saw how hiring decisions, employee experiences, and workplace culture could directly influence business outcomes.

I was hooked.

Not because of the processes.

Because of the impact.

2018
Building Without a Blueprint

Zashed Fashiontech

My first full-time role was with a startup called Zashed Fashiontech.

Like most startups, things moved fast. Very fast.

Within two months, we scaled from 7 employees to 30 employees.

There were no established frameworks, no inherited systems, and no perfect roadmap. We were building while moving.

I found myself creating hiring processes, onboarding experiences, and employee engagement initiatives from the ground up.

This experience taught me a lesson that still shapes my work today:

Growth needs structure.

Culture doesn't happen by accident. It is intentionally built.

2020
Finding My Feet in a Crisis

OSIM India

At OSIM India, I experienced one of the most challenging periods of my career.

Within a short span of time, my reporting managers moved on, and I found myself independently managing the HR function.

Then COVID happened.

As an essential healthcare services organization, shutting down was not an option. Employees came to work every day despite uncertainty, health concerns, and difficult questions that nobody had answers to.

I learned that leadership isn't always about solving every problem.

Sometimes it's about creating stability when people need it most.

Sometimes it's about listening.

Sometimes it's about simply being present.

This period taught me resilience, empathy, and the importance of clear communication during uncertainty. Most importantly, it taught me that calm leadership can make a meaningful difference in difficult times.

2021 – 2023
Discovering the Power of Data

boodmo.com

My time at boodmo.com changed how I viewed HR.

Until then, my work had largely focused on people, culture, and employee experience. At boodmo, I began exploring how data could strengthen those efforts.

Talent assessments, workforce insights, engagement trends, hiring effectiveness, and people analytics became powerful tools for understanding employee experiences more deeply.

What I discovered was simple:

Data doesn't replace empathy.

It strengthens it.

The more we understood people through meaningful insights, the better decisions we could make.

This period shaped my belief that great HR leaders balance both heart and logic.

Today
Building Happiness as Infrastructure

The Scalers

Today, I work in a fast-growing technology environment where my focus is helping create workplaces where people feel connected, supported, and empowered to grow.

I partner with leaders to improve employee experiences, strengthen manager effectiveness, build learning cultures, and create people practices that help organizations scale without losing what makes them unique.

One of the ideas I feel most strongly about is this:

Happiness at work isn't a perk.

It's infrastructure.

When people feel valued, trusted, and supported, they perform better, collaborate better, and stay longer.

Creating those environments is some of the most meaningful work I've ever done.
