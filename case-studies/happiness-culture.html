Overview

Creating a positive employee experience becomes significantly more complex when teams are distributed across geographies, cultures, and organizational boundaries.

At The Scalers, employees work closely with partner organizations across APAC, US, UK, and Europe while being employed by The Scalers. This creates a unique challenge. Employees don't just need to feel connected to their immediate team. They need to feel connected to the partner organizations they support, the leaders they collaborate with, and the culture they represent every day.

The goal was to create a workplace where employees felt connected, supported, and valued regardless of geography.

The objective was to create an experience where employees could feel connected to both The Scalers and the organizations they supported.

The happiness score became the outcome of that work.

The Challenge

The challenge was not engagement. It was identity and belonging. Employees worked daily with global stakeholders across multiple regions. They attended partner meetings, collaborated with overseas teams, and represented partner brands. Yet they were employed by a different organization.

This created a unique employee experience challenge:

How do you help employees feel connected to both organizations without feeling disconnected from either?

Additional challenges included:

  • Different communication styles across regions
  • Diverse stakeholder expectations and work cultures
  • Limited face-to-face interaction with global teams
  • Risk of employee isolation in distributed environments
  • Maintaining a consistent employee experience across teams

The objective was not simply to create engagement programs. It was to create a sense of belonging.

What I Observed

As I spent time listening to employees, managers, and partner stakeholders, one insight became clear.

The strongest employee experiences were not being created by perks or events. They were being created by connection.

Employees who felt connected to their managers, understood their role in the larger business, and had visibility into organizational decisions consistently demonstrated higher engagement and stronger commitment.

The challenge was not distance. The challenge was connection. That realization shaped every initiative that followed.

My Role

As the HR Business Partner, I partnered closely with leaders, partner managers, and employees to strengthen employee experience, cultural integration, and workplace connection.

My role focused on designing employee experiences that strengthened belonging, improved stakeholder alignment, and helped employees feel connected to both The Scalers and the organizations they supported.

Pillar 1: Strengthening Connection

These conversations helped employees feel supported throughout their journey. Actions included:

  • 15 / 30 / 60-day employee conversations

  • Employee connect programs

Pillar 2: Building Belonging

These efforts reinforced connection beyond project delivery and day-to-day work. Focus areas included:

Pillar 3: Building Global Readiness

The objective was not to change who employees were. It was to help them confidently operate in global environments through:

  • Cross-cultural communication coaching
  • Global workplace readiness sessions
  • Stakeholder management guidance
  • Understanding regional communication styles
  • Navigating diverse work expectations

Pillar 4: Increasing Leadership Visibility

Employees gained greater visibility into organizational priorities and decision-making through:

Leadership Connect Sessions Open Q&A Forums Skip-Level Conversations Business Updates & Town Halls Employee Feedback Forums

Pillar 5: Supporting Wellbeing

  • Wellbeing programs

  • Safe spaces for feedback

  • Proactive employee support

Key Learnings

Employees feel more connected when they:

  • Understand how their work contributes to larger goals
  • Have regular access to managers and leaders
  • Feel comfortable navigating different cultures
  • Receive recognition and appreciation consistently
  • Have opportunities to build meaningful relationships

The most important lesson was simple: Belonging cannot be left to chance. It must be intentionally designed.

Reflection

One of the biggest lessons from this journey was that belonging is not created by location. It is created by connection. Employees do not need to sit in the same office to feel a sense of belonging.

They need opportunities to build relationships, understand purpose, and feel seen.

When those elements exist, geography becomes far less important.

The happiness score was the outcome. Belonging was the real achievement.
